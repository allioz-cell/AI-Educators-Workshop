SOHO Cables, Connectors & Network Tools

Cabling questions almost always hide a constraint: a distance, a source of interference, a building code, or a specific symptom. Learn the limits first, terminate a jack in the correct order, then practice picking the one tool that fits the verb in the question.

Jacket ratingsPlenum cable has a fire-resistant jacket and is required in air-handling spaces above ceilings. Riser cable runs between floors. Using PVC where plenum is required is a code violation, and it shows up on the exam as a compliance question.

Terminate a jack: T568B

Pins 1, 2, 3 and 6 carry the data on 100BASE-TX; gigabit uses all four pairs.

Click the eight conductors in T568B order. The order is the one thing people forget in the lab and on the exam, and the striped wire always comes before its solid partner.

Straight-through vs crossoverA straight-through cable uses the same standard on both ends. A crossover uses T568A on one end and T568B on the other, and is nearly extinct because modern switch ports auto-negotiate with Auto-MDIX.

Fiber, coax and the rest

Match the medium to the environment: distance, interference and what the signal has to carry.

Fiber optic

  • Single-mode: tiny 9-micron core, laser light, runs for kilometers. Yellow jacket.
  • Multimode: wider 50/62.5-micron core, LED light, a few hundred meters. Orange or aqua jacket.
  • Connectors: LC (small, latching, duplex), SC (square push-pull), ST (round bayonet twist).
  • Immune to electromagnetic interference, which is why it wins any question mentioning motors, elevators or fluorescent ballasts.

Coaxial

  • RG-6: cable internet and TV, terminated with a screw-on F-connector.
  • RG-59: thinner, older, short analog video runs only.
  • A single copper core inside a braided shield — that shield is what makes it tolerant of interference.

Video and peripheral

  • HDMI: video plus audio, the consumer default.
  • DisplayPort: higher refresh rates, and one port can daisy-chain monitors.
  • DVI: video only; DVI-D digital, DVI-A analog, DVI-I both.
  • eSATA: external drives, data only — the drive still needs its own power.
