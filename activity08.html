Laser Printing: The Imaging Process

Seven stages, in a fixed order, each with its own failure signature. Once the sequence is automatic, most laser printer troubleshooting questions answer themselves — the exam describes a symptom and expects you to name the stage.

What each stage actually does

Expand any stage for the component involved and the symptom that appears when it fails.

Symptom to stage

Nearly every laser print quality question reduces to naming the stage.

SymptomStage at faultWhat to do
Toner smears or wipes off the page with a fingerFusingThe fuser is not reaching temperature. Replace the fuser assembly.
A faint copy of an earlier image repeats down the pageCleaningResidual toner is not being removed. Replace the drum or the toner cartridge that contains it.
Vertical white streaks in the same place on every pageDeveloping / exposingToner is low or something blocks the laser path. Shake the cartridge, then clean the path.
Completely blank pagesTransferringToner never reached the paper. Check the transfer roller and that the sealing tape was removed.
Random speckles and dots scattered on the pageCleaningThe cleaning blade is worn and leaving toner behind.
Paper jams at the output, often creased and warmFusingCheck for debris in the fuser and for the correct paper weight.
Multiple sheets pulled at oncePaper feedWorn pickup rollers or a worn separation pad.

Other printer types, in brief

Core 1 covers more than laser, and each type has one detail the exam likes.

TypeHow it makes a markConsumablesSignature detail
InkjetSprays liquid ink through a print headInk cartridgesRuns a calibration and head-cleaning cycle; clogs when idle for weeks
ThermalHeats special paper that darkensThermal paper onlyReceipt printers; output fades in heat and sunlight
Impact / dot matrixPins strike a ribbon against the paperRibbon, tractor-feed paperThe only technology that can print carbon-copy multipart forms
3D printerDeposits melted filament or cures resin layer by layerFilament or resinResin printing requires ventilation and gloves
Burn riskThe fuser runs around 200 °C. Let a printer cool before servicing it, and never reach into the fusing area of a printer that has just been running.
Toner handlingClean spilled toner with a vacuum rated for toner, not a shop vac — fine toner passes straight through an ordinary filter and the motor can ignite it. Wash toner off skin with cold water; hot water fuses it to you exactly as it fuses to paper.
