Hardware & Network Troubleshooting Method

The largest domain on Core 1, and the one where knowing the right fix is not enough — you have to apply it in the right order. Work four realistic tickets from first contact to documentation, choosing the action that belongs at each of the six steps.

Interactive game 40 minutes Advanced 4 tickets, 24 decisions

The methodology, in the order CompTIA scores it

Six steps. The exam tests whether you can place an action in the right one.

1

Identify the problem

Question the user, identify symptoms, and find out what changed. Back up critical data before you make any change, and ask about environmental or infrastructure changes.

2

Establish a theory of probable cause

Question the obvious first. Consider several possibilities and, if needed, conduct external or internal research based on the symptoms.

3

Test the theory to determine the cause

If the theory is confirmed, determine the next steps to resolve it. If it is not confirmed, form a new theory or escalate.

4

Establish a plan of action and implement the solution

Refer to vendor documentation. Consider corporate policies, change procedures and the impact on other users before you act.

5

Verify full system functionality

Confirm the original symptom is gone and nothing else broke. Where applicable, implement preventive measures.

6

Document findings, actions and outcomes

Write down what was wrong, what you did and what happened, so the next technician does not start from zero.

The two easy pointsTwo rules answer a surprising number of exam questions on their own. Back up before you change anything belongs to step 1, not step 4. And documentation is always last — if documenting appears as an option before the problem is verified as fixed, it is a wrong answer.
